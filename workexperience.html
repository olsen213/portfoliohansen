Factory Production

I have worked at several production line jobs and have learned to work efficiently under pressure while staying calm. Two companies I have worked for that have taught me this are Lowers Food Inc, and Pepperidge Farm. At both places, I had to meet quota's as a team and working together was crucial to our overall success. I learned to be aware of the environment around me and to step into uncomfortable situations to complete unexpected tasks when they came up. Other aspects of these jobs included constantly looking for ways to improve as a company. I can better identify problems quickly and correct them before causing bigger issues further on the line because of these companies.

House Framing

I have worked on building frameworks for houses which have taught me practical skills needed to be successful in this work area. While working with house-framing, I got to handle power tools, use various units of measurement, and listen to contractors in the planning and execution of the day. I would also look at blue prints of the houses from time to time and work with other builders to make sure we were following the plan. A typical day included making stairs, putting up siding for various walls, and using pressurized tools for building with precision. Through this, I learned the value of endurance and moving past mistakes in order to be successful. Making mistakes was part of the building process and always brought new opportunities to learn from. I really enjoyed my time building houses.
